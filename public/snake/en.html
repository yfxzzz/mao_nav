Game Instructions

Game Background

Snake is a classic casual game that originated from the 1976 arcade game "Blockade". It gained worldwide popularity in the 1990s with the spread of Nokia phones and became a memory for a generation. The core gameplay is simple yet challenging: players control a continuously moving snake, eating food to grow while avoiding collisions with walls or its own body.

Basic Rules

1. The snake moves forward automatically, and players can only control its direction (up, down, left, right).

2. When the snake eats food, its body grows by one segment, and the score increases.

3. If the snake hits a wall or its own body, the game ends.

4. There is no time limit; the goal is to achieve the highest possible score.

High Score Tips

1. Plan your route: Try to make the snake move in large circles within the game area, avoiding sharp turns.

2. Use the boundaries: You can use the game area boundaries to change direction and avoid hitting yourself.

3. Control the pace: Move quickly when the snake is short, but slow down and operate carefully when it grows longer.

4. Stay calm: Don't panic in dangerous situations; calmly think about the best direction to move.

5. Practice reactions: Improve your reaction speed and operational precision through continuous practice.

Difficulty Settings

The game offers three difficulty levels:

- Easy: The snake moves at a slower speed, suitable for beginners.

- Medium: The snake moves at a moderate speed, suitable for players with some experience.

- Hard: The snake moves at a faster speed, suitable for challenging players seeking a challenge.
